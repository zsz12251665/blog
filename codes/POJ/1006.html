Solution

To solve this problem, you need to know a little about the Chinese remainder theorem. It explains how to solve congruent equations.

First of all, we need to find out the number-theoretic reciprocal of 23, 28 and 33 at first. They are 6, 19, and 2. And then according to the Chinese remainder theorem, the day which is 6*28*33*p+19*23*33*e+2*23*28*i days from the first day of the year must be a triple peak occured day. And then we decuct the days that had passed, and take the remainer which devided with 23*28*33, we'll get the next triple peak occured day. So keep it in one, the answer is (5544*p+14421*e+1288*i-d+21252-1) mod 21252+1(to add 21252 is to keep the devided number is greater than 0 and to -1 and +1 is to keep the answer in [1,21252]).

If you have problem with some the number-theoretic reciprocal, here may give you some reference: To a pair of (a,m)(a and m are integers), if there is an integer n fixes the equation a*n≡1(mod m), we call the number n the number-theoretic reciprocal of (a,m). For example, because 6*28*33≡1(mod 23), so 6 is the number-theoretic reciprocal of (28*33,23).

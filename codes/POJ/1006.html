Solution

To solve this problem, you need to know a little about the Chinese remainder theorem. It explains how to solve congruent equations.

First of all, we need to find out the number-theoretic reciprocal of c{23}, c{28} and c{33} at first. They are c{6}, c{19} and c{2}. And then according to the Chinese remainder theorem, the day which is o{(}o{(}c{6}o{×}c{28}o{×}c{33}o{)}o{}v{p}o{+}o{(}c{23}o{×}c{19}o{×}c{33}o{)}o{}v{e}o{+}o{(}c{23}o{×}c{28}o{×}c{2}o{)}o{}v{i}o{)} days from the first day of the year must be a triple peak occurred day. And then we deduct the days that had passed, and take the remainder which is divided with c{23}o{×}c{28}o{×}c{33}, we'll get the next triple peak occurred day. So keep it in one, the answer is o{(}c{5544}o{}v{p}o{+}c{14421}o{}v{e}o{+}c{1288}o{}v{i}o{-}v{d}o{+}c{21252}o{-}c{1}o{)}o{mod}c{21252}o{+}c{1} (to add a c{21252} is to keep the answer greater than 0, to minus 1 and then plus 1 is to keep the answer in o{[}c{1}o{,}c{21252}o{]}).

If you have problem with some the number-theoretic reciprocal, here may give you some reference: To a pair of o{(}v{a}o{,}v{n}o{)}o{(}v{a}o{,}v{m}o{∈}c{ℕ}b{o{+}}o{)}, if there is an integer v{n} which satisfies v{a}o{}v{n}o{≡}c{1}o{(}o{mod}v{m}o{)}, we call v{n} the number-theoretic reciprocal of o{(}v{a}o{,}v{m}o{)}. For example, because c{6}o{×}c{28}o{×}c{33}o{≡}c{1}o{(}o{mod}c{23}o{)}, so c{6} is the number-theoretic reciprocal of o{(}c{28}o{×}c{33}o{,}c{23}o{)}.
